# French Style Green Beans: The Perfect Side Dish

If you're looking to add a touch of elegance to your dinner table, French style green beans are a delightful choice. Also known as haricot verts, these slender and tender beans are a staple in French cuisine. With their vibrant color, refined flavor, and versatile nature, they are a wonderful addition to any meal.

## Taste and Texture

French style green beans have a distinct taste compared to their regular counterparts. They offer a slightly sweeter and more delicate flavor. The texture is crisp, yet tender, making them a pleasure to eat. These beans often have a satisfying "snap" when you bite into them, providing a satisfying mouthfeel.

## Common Uses in Cooking

These beans are incredibly versatile and can be used in a myriad of culinary preparations. They are commonly blanched and tossed into salads, stir-fried with other vegetables, or served as a simple and elegant side dish. One classic French preparation involves sautéing them briefly in butter, resulting in a rich and flavorful accompaniment to meat or fish.

Moreover, French style green beans can be dressed up with various additions. Try caramelizing shallots, sprinkling toasted almonds, or adding a drizzle of tangy vinaigrette to elevate their taste. The possibilities are endless, allowing you to get creative in your kitchen.

## Nutritional Value

Apart from their delicious taste, French style green beans also offer a range of health benefits. They are low in calories and fat, making them an excellent choice for those watching their weight. They are also packed with essential vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, vitamin K, and potassium. Additionally, being a good source of dietary fiber, they contribute to a healthy digestive system.

## A French Culinary Tradition

The popularity of French style green beans dates back centuries. The French have long embraced the concept of simplicity and fresh ingredients, and these beans perfectly embody this culinary philosophy. They became especially popular during the 19th century, when the "haute cuisine" movement emphasized the importance of visually appealing dishes.

Interestingly, the term "haricot verts" is the French translation for "green beans." The French have designated this specific variety as worthy of a distinct name due to its exceptional taste and visual appeal.

## Fun Fact

Did you know that French style green beans were once considered a luxurious delicacy? Until the 18th century, these beans were only grown for ornamental purposes in French gardens. It wasn't until later that their fantastic taste and versatility were fully recognized.

## Conclusion

French style green beans are a delightful addition to any meal. With their refined flavor, crisp texture, and rich history, they bring an element of sophistication to your plate. Whether you're serving them as a side dish or incorporating them into a more elaborate recipe, these slender green beans are sure to impress your guests and elevate your cooking to new heights.

### French Style Green Beans

- **Origin**: French style green beans, also known as Haricots Verts, originated in France. The term "Haricots Verts" is French for "green beans."

- **Common Uses**: French style green beans are often used in various culinary applications. They can be steamed, blanched, sautéed, stir-fried, or roasted. They are commonly featured in salads, side dishes, and as a vegetable component in various main courses.

- **Nutritional Benefits**: French style green beans are low in calories and fat, making them a nutritious addition to meals. They are a good source of dietary fiber, vitamin C, vitamin K, and folate. They also contain some iron, potassium, and antioxidants.

- **Unique Properties**: French style green beans are thinner and more tender than regular green beans. They have a crisp texture and offer a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Their slender shape gives them an elegant appearance on the plate.

- **Historical Significance**: French style green beans have been a part of French cuisine for centuries. The French cooking technique of blanching these beans involves briefly boiling them in water and then immediately plunging them into ice water to preserve their vibrant green color. This method of cooking is said to have originated in France.

- **Alternative Names**: In addition to French style green beans and Haricots Verts, these beans are also sometimes referred to as French beans, fine beans, or string beans.

Remember to incorporate these facts into your cooking or discussions about French style green beans!
